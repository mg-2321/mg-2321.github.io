I’m a second‑year M.S. student in the Data Science department at the University of Washington, Seattle. My research focuses on agentic AI: planning and teaching language models when to ask vs. when to act, with lightweight grounding via a knowledge‑augmented graph (KAG) and preference‑based training (SFT → per‑step DPO/SimPO; optional KTO/ORPO for asymmetric ask/act risk).

I also work on overcoming context‑window limits using short‑ and long‑term retrieval caches, summarization/compression, and Reflexion‑style episodic notes that are indexed and reused across tasks.

I’m open to research collaborations—please email gayat23@uw.edu—especially on assistive agents (day‑to‑day task automation, tool use, XR copilots) and self‑evolving language models (curriculum generation, reflective fine‑tuning, preference optimization).
