I’m a second-year M.S. student in the Comuter Science department at the University of Washington. Currently, my research supervised by Dr. Ruta Desai focuses on agentic AI: planning and teaching language models when to ask vs. when to act, with lightweight grounding via a knowledge-augmented graph (KAG). Additionally, preference-based training (SFT → per-step DPO/SimPO; optional KTO/ORPO for asymmetric <Ask>/<Act> risk).

I also work on overcoming context-window limits using short- and long-term retrieval caches, summarization/compression, and Reflexion-style episodic notes that are indexed and reused across tasks.

I’m open to research collaborations. Please email me (gayat23 [at] uw.edu) if you are interested to explore on on assistive agents for day-to-day task automation, tool use, XR copilots, and self-evolving language models for curriculum generation, reflective fine-tuning, and preference optimization.
